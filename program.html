College of Technology

Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering

The Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering (BSCE) program integrates principles of electrical engineering and computer science to prepare students for designing and developing computer hardware and software systems. The curriculum covers topics such as digital systems design, microprocessors, computer architecture, embedded systems, software development, and network security. Students gain hands-on experience through laboratory work, design projects, and internships, equipping them with practical skills and problem-solving abilities.

Bachelor of Science in Information Technology

The Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSIT) program is designed to equip students with the essential skills and knowledge needed to excel in the field of information technology. The curriculum covers a wide range of topics, including computer programming, network administration, database management, cybersecurity, and systems analysis.

Bachelor of Science in Information System

The Bachelor of Science in Information Systems (BSIS) program focuses on the intersection of business and technology, preparing students to design, implement, and manage information systems that support organizational operations and decision-making. The curriculum includes courses in systems analysis and design, database management, business process management, project management, and cybersecurity.

Bachelor of Science in Computer Science

The Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (BSCS) program focuses on the theoretical foundations and practical applications of computing. Students in this program learn fundamental concepts in programming, algorithms, data structures, software engineering, and computer systems. The curriculum emphasizes both theoretical understanding and hands-on experience, preparing students for careers in software development, computer systems analysis, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and more.

College of Accountancy

Bachelor of Science in Accountancy

The Bachelor of Science in Accountancy (BSA) program provides students with a comprehensive understanding of accounting principles, financial reporting, auditing, and taxation. The curriculum covers topics such as financial accounting, managerial accounting, accounting information systems, and business law. Students develop skills in analyzing financial data, preparing financial statements, and ensuring regulatory compliance.
